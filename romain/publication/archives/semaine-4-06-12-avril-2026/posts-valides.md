```
A conversation I had recently changed how I think about e-commerce analytics entirely.

For context, I've been shifting my focus from dev tools to e-commerce over the past few weeks. The developer market taught me a lot, but the pain I'm finding in the Shopify ecosystem is on a completely different scale.

Case in point.

A merchant was frustrated. He had what looked like strong traffic numbers. Six figures in daily visitors. His conversion rate was sitting at 0.3%. He had tested everything. New creatives, different landing pages, adjusted targeting, increased spend. Nothing moved the needle.

The problem was not his funnel. The problem was his traffic.

When he finally investigated, he discovered that the vast majority of his visitors were automated bots. Over three million hits in a single month, concentrated on just two pages. His real human traffic was a fraction of what his dashboard reported.

The implications were significant. His conversion rate was not actually 0.3%. It was closer to 4% when calculated against real visitors. His ad optimization was being informed by polluted data. He was scaling spend to attract more of an audience that did not exist.

This is not an isolated case. Bot traffic on Shopify stores is far more prevalent than most merchants realize. The signals to watch for: abnormally high bounce rates above 85%, session durations under 10 seconds at scale, and traffic spikes that do not correlate with any campaign or content activity.

Before optimizing your conversion funnel, it may be worth verifying that your traffic is real.

Have you ever audited the quality of your store traffic?
```
