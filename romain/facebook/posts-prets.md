## J20 (ven 25/04) — Shopify Entrepreneurs FB — 1er post

I've been doing free conversion audits on Shopify stores for a few weeks. Not as a service, more as a learning exercise to understand what separates stores that convert at 3% from those stuck at 0.5%.

After looking at ~40 stores, the single most common issue isn't what I expected. It's not bad products, bad photos, or bad pricing.

It's trust. Specifically — the store looks like it could be a scam. No real about page, no return policy visible on product pages, no reviews, and no way to contact a human.

If you're struggling with conversions, before you spend money on ads or redesign, ask yourself: would YOU buy from your store if you'd never heard of it?

What's the one change that made the biggest difference to your conversion rate? Curious to hear what worked for people here.

## J28 (sam 03/05) — Shopify Newbies FB — Post suivi

Quick tip that came from auditing 30+ stores this month: if your store loads slower than 3 seconds on mobile, nothing else matters. People leave before they even see your product.

Fastest fix: count how many Shopify apps you have installed. If it's more than 10, start removing the ones you don't actively use. Each app injects code that slows everything down.

Second fastest: check your images. If any product image is over 500KB, compress it. Tons of free tools do this.

These two changes alone can cut 2-3 seconds off your load time. It's not sexy but it works.
