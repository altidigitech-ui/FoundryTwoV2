## 2. VOIX R SUR TWITTER

### 2.1 Principes

R est une **personne**, pas un studio. Il parle au "I", jamais au "we" (sauf quand il parle de F2 explicitement). Le ton est celui d'un expert growth e-com & marketing : direct, provocateur, data-driven, avec une pointe d'humour sec. R donne des opinions tranchees. R n'est pas neutre — c'est ce qui le differencie de F2.

### 2.2 Positionnement

R se positionne comme un **expert growth e-com & marketing**, pas un theoricien. Tout ce qu'il dit est ancre dans les donnees terrain (530+ reviews, chargebacks, Reddit, patterns merchants). Il ne repete pas des best practices generiques — il partage ce qu'il voit dans les donnees reelles du terrain.

### 2.3 Regles de voix

| Regle | Exemple OK | Exemple interdit |
|-------|-----------|------------------|
| Parler au "I" | "I talked to 50+ merchants this month. $800/month in chargebacks they don't even know about." | "We talked to 50+ merchants..." (c'est la voix F2) |
| Opinions tranchees | "Your agency charges $5K/month but can't show which campaign drove the last sale." | "Maybe consider reviewing your agency's reporting?" (trop mou) |
| Provocateur mais constructif | "Most Shopify stores are bleeding $800/month in chargebacks. 71% isn't even real fraud. The data backs this up." | "Shopify is a scam." (attaque gratuite) |
| Toujours un insight ou une donnee | "I analyzed 530+ reviews this week. Pattern: merchants don't know what their apps actually cost them." | "Hidden costs are bad." (generique) |
| Jamais de pitch direct pour les produits | "Looked at the chargeback data. 71% of disputes aren't real fraud. Here's what's actually happening:" | "Check out our chargeback tool! Best solution!" |
| Humour sec, pas de LOL | "Your store has 14 apps installed. Congrats, that's $400/month in app tax before your first sale." | "LMAO your store is so bloated" |
| Jamais de vocabulaire forge | Vocabulaire growth/e-com standard | "Forged a new insight" (c'est la voix F2) |

### 2.4 Vocabulaire R

| Autorise | Interdit |
|----------|---------|
| I, my, I've seen, in my experience | we, our, the studio (sauf reference explicite a F2) |
| chargeback, store speed, hidden cost, client ROI, app tax, conversion, funnel | forged, hammered, from the forge, anvil |
| data shows, patterns, I've talked to X merchants | revolutionary, game-changing, unprecedented |
| here's what I found, here's the problem | check out our tool, you should try [produit] |
| hot take, unpopular opinion, fight me | like and share if you agree, RT if you think |

### 2.5 Ce que R peut couvrir

R CAN talk about chargebacks, store speed, hidden costs, app bloat, agency ROI — c'est du business, pas du technique pur. La separation avec F est : R = angle business/argent/ROI, F = angle technique/code/architecture.

### 2.6 Emojis

Max 1 par tweet, souvent aucun. Emojis acceptables : (insight), (data), (thread). Jamais : des emojis excessifs type fusee, feu, diamant, etc.

## 4. COLD OUTREACH TWITTER — ANGLE R

Le cold outreach est defini en detail dans marketing/context.md §8. Ici on precise les regles **Twitter-specifiques** pour R.

### 4.1 Rappel du process

1. **Grok** trouve les cibles (prompt `GROK-R-COLDOUTREACH`) → 15 min
2. **Donnees terrain** fournissent les insights terrain (chargebacks, reviews, patterns) → 10 min
3. **Claude** (projet redaction R) redige la reply unique → 15 min
4. **R** publie la reply en public depuis @delgado_ro72224

### 4.2 L'angle R dans le cold outreach

R s'interesse aux problemes **BUSINESS** du merchant ou de l'agence : chargebacks caches, app tax, store speed qui coute des ventes, ROI client impossible a prouver, hidden costs. Jamais aux problemes techniques (code, performance back-end, stack) — c'est l'angle de F.

### 4.3 Criteres de ciblage Twitter

| Critere | Valeur |
|---------|--------|
| Anciennete du post | < 48h |
| Type de cible | Merchants Shopify, agency owners, e-com founders |
| Taille du compte | 50 - 50K followers |
| Nombre de reponses existantes | < 50 (au-dela, la reply est noyee) |
| Type de post | Plainte chargebacks, question ROI, "losing money", partage metriques, probleme agence |
| Exclusions | Comptes > 100K, Fortune 500, posts purement techniques |

### 4.4 Termes de recherche Grok (onglet "Latest")

```
"shopify chargeback"
"losing money shopify"
"client reporting nightmare"
"prove ROI client"
"agency billing"
"hidden costs shopify"
"shopify apps cost"
"chargebacks killing"
"client churn agency"
"can't prove ROI"
"shopify store slow"
"too many apps shopify"
"agency client retention"
"e-commerce hidden fees"
```

### 4.5 Templates cold outreach

**Template e-com :**

```
Your store is probably losing $800/month to chargebacks.
71% of it isn't even real fraud.
Here's what I'd fix first:
→ [Insight 1 from données terrain]
→ [Insight 2]
→ [Insight 3]
Happy to dig deeper if useful.
```

**Template agency :**

```
I talked to 50+ agency owners.
The #1 pain: proving ROI to clients.
Here's what the top ones do differently:
→ [Insight 1]
→ [Insight 2]
→ [Insight 3]
```

Le lien vers plus de details va en **reply sous cette reply** (pas dans le meme tweet — le premier tweet capte l'engagement, le lien suit).

### 4.6 Regles absolues du cold outreach R

1. **Toujours en reponse publique**, jamais en DM en premier.
2. **Chaque reply est une mini-pub organique** vue par tous ceux qui lisent le fil.
3. **Ne jamais pitcher un produit par nom dans la reply.** "data analysis" ou "chargeback audit" suffisent.
4. **Ne jamais copier-coller.** Chaque reply est unique (l'algo detecte les patterns).
5. **10 outreachs minimum par jour.** Non negociable.
6. **Si la cible repond** → repondre dans les 2h, approfondir, proposer d'aider. C'est le debut du funnel.

## 6. CONTENU R — TYPES ET EXEMPLES

Les formats sont definis dans twitter/context.md §6. Ici on precise le **contenu specifique R**.

### 6.1 Hot Take R (1-2x/semaine)

Angle : opinion tranchee sur un sujet e-com/agencies que les gens ne peuvent pas ignorer. Structure : affirmation brutale → developpement → question ouverte (cf. growth-marketing/context.md §3.2).

```
Your Shopify store has 14 apps installed.

That's $400/month in "app tax" before you sell a single product.
Half of them overlap. A third of them slow your store down.

But sure, let's talk about your ad spend.

How many apps are you running right now?
```

```
Unpopular opinion: Chargebacks are a bigger problem than low traffic for most Shopify stores.

$800/month average. 71% isn't even real fraud.
But nobody tracks it because it's "just a cost of doing business".

It's not. It's a leak.
```

### 6.2 Data post R (1-2x/semaine)

Angle : pattern observe dans les donnees terrain de la semaine. Toujours ancre dans des donnees reelles.

```
I analyzed 530+ merchant reviews this month.

The #1 hidden cost: chargebacks they don't even know about.

Average: $800/month. 71% aren't real fraud.
The merchants who fix this see margins jump 15-20%.

What's your biggest hidden cost right now?
```

### 6.3 Question R (2-3x/semaine)

Angle : question qui provoque une vraie conversation. Issue des patterns du cold outreach ou des debats de la communaute.

```
Agency owners:

What's the #1 metric your clients ask about that you struggle to prove?

(And do you actually have the data, or are you winging it?)
```

```
Shopify merchants:

Do you know your ACTUAL chargeback rate?
Not the Shopify dashboard number — the real one including hidden disputes.

Drop it below. I bet 80% of you don't know.
```

### 6.4 Cold outreach public (quotidien — via le process §4)

C'est le format le plus frequent de R. Chaque cold outreach = un tweet public avec des donnees reelles. Les meilleurs cold outreachs sont eux-memes du contenu (cf. marketing/context.md §8.8).

### 6.5 Thread R (1x/semaine max)

Angle : un learning du cold outreach, un pattern profond, ou un guide actionnable e-com/agencies.

```
Tweet 1: I've talked to 50+ Shopify merchants in the last 2 weeks.

Here are the 5 hidden costs bleeding their stores dry (thread)

Tweet 2: Hidden cost #1 — Chargebacks they don't track.
Average: $800/month. 71% isn't real fraud.
Most merchants just accept it as "cost of doing business".

Tweet 3: Hidden cost #2 — App bloat.
14 apps average. $400/month. Half overlap.
Your store loads in 6 seconds instead of 2. That's 30% of visitors gone.

[...]

Tweet 5: What's the biggest hidden cost YOU've found in your store?
```

## 9. BATCH ET WORKFLOW DE REDACTION R

### 9.1 Qui redige quoi

| Tache | Qui | Outil |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Posts R perso (5-7/semaine) | R | Projet Claude redaction R |
| Cold outreach replies | R | Projet Claude redaction R (donnees Grok + terrain data en input) |
| Cross-engagement replies | R | Manuel (pas besoin de Claude pour une reply courte) |
| Posts F2 (5/semaine) | R | Projet Claude redaction F2 (voix differente, cf. f2/context.md) |

### 9.2 Process batch samedi

1. **Vendredi soir — Reunion F+R** (1h) : revue Google Sheet, top/worst posts, decisions, inputs de F.
2. **Samedi soir — Redaction R** (1-2h) :
   - Ouvrir le projet Claude redaction R
   - Fournir : patterns de la semaine (cold outreach), donnees terrain, sujets tendance de la niche
   - Claude genere 5-7 posts dans la voix R
   - R valide, ajuste, programme
3. **Samedi soir — Redaction F2** (1h) : switch vers le projet Claude redaction F2 (cf. f2/context.md §7.2).

### 9.3 En semaine

Zero redaction de posts. Execution uniquement : publier, cross-engagement, repondre aux replies. Les cold outreach replies sont les seuls contenus rediges en live (pas batchable — depend des cibles du jour).

## 10. EVOLUTION DU CONTENU R

### Phase 1 — Avril : Warming e-com + cold outreach insights

- **Dominante :** Cold outreach public e-com + hot takes pour etablir la voix.
- **Mix :** 40% provocation, 30% questions, 20% cold outreach public, 10% build in public perso.
- **Objectif :** que les gens reconnaissent R comme "le gars qui trouve ce qui coute de l'argent aux merchants — et qui le dit cash".

### Phase 2 — Mai : Expansion agencies (R lead) + data posts multi-vertical

- **Dominante :** Data posts enrichis (volume de donnees terrain suffisant) + expansion agencies. R lead sur la verticale agencies.
- **Mix :** 30% data, 25% provocation, 25% cold outreach public, 20% questions.
- **Nouveau format :** "Audit My Store" — R invite les merchants a soumettre leur URL, audite en public. Aimant a engagement + genere des cibles volontaires.

### Phase 3 — Juin+ : Autorite "expert growth multi-vertical"

- **Dominante :** Autorite installee, threads profonds, guest audits multi-vertical.
- **Mix :** 35% data, 25% communaute, 20% provocation, 20% cold outreach.
- **Nouveau :** Guest audits avec des experts e-com/agency Tier 1, debats structures, contenu plus ambitieux. R = reference growth multi-vertical (e-com + agencies + conversion).
