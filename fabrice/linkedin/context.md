## 2. VOIX F SUR LINKEDIN

### 2.1 Principes

F sur LinkedIn est un builder qui automatise des problèmes business (e-com + creators). Sur Twitter, F est concis et direct. Sur LinkedIn, F **développe le raisonnement technique de manière accessible** — le fond est technique mais l'angle est toujours orienté vers le problème business qu'il résout. Le fond est identique, la forme est professionnelle et pédagogique.

### 2.2 Différences Twitter vs LinkedIn pour F

| Aspect | Twitter F | LinkedIn F |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Ton | Concis, war stories, punchlines techniques | Pédagogique, raisonnement développé, tradeoffs expliqués |
| Longueur | 100-260 caractères | 800-1300 caractères |
| Structure | 1 bloc percutant ou thread | Texte aéré, 1 phrase/ligne, hook + développement + question |
| Bug stories | "Store loads in 4s. 14 apps. Fixed." (3 lignes) | "Here's why your store loads in 4 seconds, what causes it, and a 5-minute diagnostic to fix it." (10 lignes) |
| Diagnostics | "14 Shopify apps = 14 JavaScript injections = 3 extra seconds of load time. Period." | "The reason your store loads in 4s: here's what each app does under the hood." |
| CTA | Question technique directe | Question ouverte professionnelle |
| Lien | Jamais dans le tweet | Jamais dans le post. Le profil fait le travail. |
| Vocabulaire forge | Rarement | Jamais. Ton professionnel standard. |
| Émojis | 0-1 max | 0-1 max, professionnels (⚡, 🔨) |

### 2.3 Règles de voix LinkedIn F

| Règle | Exemple OK | Exemple interdit |
|-------|-----------|------------------|
| Parler au "I" | "I built an agent that automates chargeback evidence collection. 4 hours → 30 seconds." | "We built..." (voix F2 studio) |
| Technique et accessible | "The average Shopify store runs 14 apps. Each injects its own JavaScript. That's 200-500ms per app. Here's the diagnostic I run." | "We had a performance issue." (trop vague pour LinkedIn) |
| Raisonnement explicite | "The reason your store loads in 4s isn't the images — it's the apps. Here's what each one does under the hood." | "Apps are slow." (affirmation sans raisonnement) |
| Actionnable | "Open your page source. Search for <script>. Count them. If there's more than 8, that's your bottleneck." | "You should check your store speed." (générique) |
| Jamais de pitch | "The hardest part of building an automated chargeback defense is collecting evidence in real time — each dispute has a 7-day window." | "Try ProfitPilot for automated protection!" |
| Jamais de lien dans le post | Valeur dans le post, foundrytwo.com dans la bio | Lien dans le corps ou en commentaire |

## 4. ADAPTATION TWITTER → LINKEDIN

F publie sur les mêmes sujets techniques sur les deux plateformes. Chaque post est **réécrit** pour LinkedIn.

### 4.1 Exemple concret

**Tweet F (Twitter) :**
```
Your Shopify store loads in 4.2s.

The bottleneck: 14 apps each injecting their own JS.

Quick win: audit your <script> tags. If there's more than 8, start uninstalling.
```

**Post F (LinkedIn) — même sujet, format adapté :**
```
Why your Shopify store loads in 4.2 seconds — and a 5-minute diagnostic

I've been analyzing store performance for the past month. The pattern is always the same: it's not the images, it's not the theme. It's the apps.

The average Shopify store runs 14 apps. Each one injects its own JavaScript. Each injection adds 200-500ms of load time. That's 2.8 to 7 seconds of extra load time from apps alone.

Here's the diagnostic I run:

Open your page source. Search for <script>. Count them. If there's more than 8, that's your bottleneck.

The top 3 offenders I keep seeing: [app 1], [app 2], [app 3].

Quick fix: uninstall any app you haven't used in 30 days. Then check your load time again.

What's your <script> count? Curious to hear.
```

### 4.2 Règle de transformation

| Élément | Twitter → LinkedIn |
|---------|-------------------|
| Diagnostic en 3 lignes ("14 apps = slow store") | Développement complet : le problème → la cause technique → les données → le diagnostic → le fix |
| Punchline technique ("Quick win: audit your tags") | Guide step-by-step reproductible par le lecteur |
| Question directe ("Start uninstalling") | Question ouverte professionnelle ("What's your count? Curious to hear.") |
| 200 caractères | 800-1300 caractères |
| Zéro contexte (le tweet se suffit) | Contexte complet (combien de stores analysés, pattern récurrent, données) |

## 5. CONTENU F — TYPES ET EXEMPLES LINKEDIN

### 5.1 Texte long — format par défaut (2-3x/semaine)

```
Most Shopify "speed optimization" apps make your store SLOWER.

I know this sounds backwards. But after analyzing 50+ stores, the pattern is always the same.

Here's what happens:

→ You install a speed app to optimize images and lazy load
→ The app itself injects JavaScript to do its job
→ That JavaScript adds 200-500ms of load time
→ You now have an optimization app that adds latency

The average Shopify store runs 14 apps. Each one injects its own JS. That's 2.8 to 7 seconds of extra load time from apps alone.

The irony: the solution to store speed isn't more apps. It's fewer apps.

Quick diagnostic: open your page source. Count the <script> tags. If there's more than 8, start there.

We're building an AI agent that automates this diagnostic. But the manual fix takes 5 minutes.

What's the app you installed that you regret the most?
```

### 5.2 Document / Carousel (PDF) — format premium (Phase 2+)

Quand l'utiliser : diagnostics techniques, workflow automation step-by-step, comparaison d'approches.

**Exemple structure carousel F (7 slides) :**

| Slide | Contenu |
|-------|---------|
| 1 (couverture) | "How StoreMD Detects App Conflicts on Shopify Stores — The Full Diagnostic Pipeline" |
| 2 | Step 1 : Analyze page source — count <script> injections per app. |
| 3 | Step 2 : Measure load impact per app — waterfall analysis. |
| 4 | Step 3 : Detect conflicts between apps (CSS overrides, JS errors). |
| 5 | Step 4 : Generate priority list — which apps to keep, which to remove. |
| 6 | The hard part : making the diagnostic automated and accurate across 1000s of themes. |
| 7 (conclusion) | "The average store can cut 2-3 seconds of load time in 10 minutes. The diagnostic is the bottleneck, not the fix." + "How many apps are you running right now?" |

Produit avec Canva Pro. Template cohérent. Mobile first.

### 5.3 Question ouverte (1x/semaine)

```
Shopify merchants: how many apps do you run on your store?

I've been analyzing store performance and the average is 14. Each one adds load time.

But here's the thing — some apps are worth the tradeoff. Others are ghost billing you after you stopped using them.

What's your count, and which app do you wish you'd never installed?
```

### 5.4 Storytelling technique (1x toutes les 2 semaines)

```
I automated chargeback evidence collection. Here's what happened.

The average Shopify merchant spends 4 hours per dispute collecting evidence: order details, shipping tracking, customer communication, delivery confirmation.

They have 7 days to respond. Most miss the deadline.

So I built an agent that does it in 30 seconds:
→ Detects the dispute notification
→ Pulls all evidence from Shopify, shipping API, email logs
→ Formats it into the exact template the bank requires
→ Submits within hours, not days

The technical challenge wasn't the AI — it was the integrations. Each payment processor has a different dispute format. Each bank wants different evidence.

The result: win rate went from 15% (industry average for merchants who respond) to [X]% with automated evidence.

If you're building integrations with payment processors, what's been your biggest headache?
```

### 6.3 Volume et exemples de commentaires

15 interactions stratégiques/jour, lun-ven (cf. linkedin/context.md §3.8).

**Commentaire F :**
"Classic scaling problem. I've been diagnosing this pattern on 50+ stores. 90% of the time it's not your hosting — it's your app stack. Each app injects its own JavaScript and processes its own server-side requests. During a sale, the traffic spike doesn't just hit Shopify's servers — it hits every app's API simultaneously. Quick diagnostic: check your app count. If it's above 12, start by disabling any app you don't actively use during sales. How many apps are you running?"

**Exemple — Post trouvé :** "Content creators: I spend more time editing than creating. Something needs to change."

I've been studying this pattern: top creators spend 20% of their time creating and 80% distributing. The fix isn't better editing — it's automating the distribution chain. One video → extract key moments → generate clips → adapt format per platform → schedule. That whole chain can be automated. How many platforms are you distributing to right now?"

## 7. ROUTINE QUOTIDIENNE LINKEDIN DE F

LinkedIn s'intègre dans le planning quotidien de F.

### 7.1 Temps alloué

F est full-time. LinkedIn fait partie des 30 interactions quotidiennes (split Twitter + LinkedIn). Les 15 interactions LinkedIn s'intègrent naturellement dans la journée.

### 7.2 Détail quotidien LinkedIn

| Étape | Action |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | Publier le post LinkedIn du jour (préparé au batch). Si pas de post ce jour → skip. |
| 2 | Engagement à valeur : 15 interactions stratégiques sur des posts pertinents. |
| 3 | Cross-engagement : commenter le dernier post de R sur LinkedIn (si R a publié). |

### 7.3 Créneau de publication

F publie aux horaires optimaux algo (cf. linkedin/context.md §6.2). Adapter en fonction de la journée.

### 7.4 Flexibilité

Si le temps manque :
- **Minimum vital :** cross-engagement R = ne jamais couper le cross-engagement.
- **À couper en premier :** la publication (le post peut attendre demain — LinkedIn pardonne les pauses) puis une partie de l'engagement à valeur.

## 8. BATCH ET WORKFLOW DE RÉDACTION

### 8.1 Qui rédige quoi

| Tâche | Qui | Outil |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Posts LinkedIn F (2-3/semaine) | F | Projet Claude rédaction F |
| Carousels (Phase 2+) | F | Canva Pro |
| Cross-engagement | F | Manuel |

### 8.2 Comment F rédige

F rédige ses propres posts :

1. **Vendredi soir — Réunion F+R** : F fournit ses inputs techniques de la semaine.
2. **F rédige ses posts LinkedIn** quand il veut (vendredi, samedi, ou au fil de la semaine). Pas de contrainte horaire imposée.
3. **Outil :** Projet Claude rédaction F. F fournit ses notes techniques brutes → Claude rédige dans la voix LinkedIn F → F valide et ajuste.
4. **Transformation Twitter → LinkedIn :** F identifie les meilleurs sujets de la semaine (communs à Twitter et LinkedIn). Claude transforme le format : allonger à 800-1300 caractères, développer le raisonnement, ajouter le contexte, reformuler le hook, question professionnelle de clôture.

### 8.3 Règle anti-duplication

Chaque post LinkedIn de F doit passer le test : "Si quelqu'un suit F sur Twitter ET LinkedIn, est-ce qu'il voit du contenu différent ?" Si le post ressemble trop au tweet → réécrire. Même sujet OK, même formulation interdit.
