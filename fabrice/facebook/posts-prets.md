## J20 (ven 25/04) — Shopify Newbies FB — 1er post

Quick technical tip that I wish someone had told me when I started: your Shopify store probably has apps installed that you forgot about, and they're slowing everything down.

Go to Settings > Apps and count them. If you have more than 10, start removing the ones you haven't used in the last month. Each one loads its own code on every page visit, even if you're not using it.

I tested this on about 50 stores recently and the average was 14 apps installed. Removing unused ones typically saves 1-3 seconds of load time. That's huge.

Your visitors won't wait. Google says 53% of people leave if a page takes more than 3 seconds to load on mobile. Check your store on your phone right now — how long does it take?

## J28 (sam 03/05) — Shopify Newbies FB — Post suivi

Most underrated Shopify optimization: image compression. I audited 30+ stores this week and found that product images were the #2 performance killer after app bloat.

Most people upload images straight from their camera or supplier — 4000x4000px, 2-5MB each. Shopify displays them at ~800px max. That's 5-25x more data than needed, loaded on every page.

Fix: compress every product image to under 200KB before uploading. TinyPNG does this for free. Your pages will load 2-3 seconds faster without changing anything else.
